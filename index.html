About

I recently completed my MSci at Glasgow with a First, specialising in astrophysics and theoretical physics. My master's research used both classical numerical methods and physics-informed neural networks to study perturbations of Schwarzschild black holes. Alongside physics I've run the finances of the University of Glasgow Physics Society, taught Python and LaTeX to younger students and organised large events including the annual UofG science ball (with 500+ attendees in 2025). Now, I'm working on the quantitative backbone of Klovr — the Klovr passport score. This is a scoring model which separates luck from skill and decides which traders earn funded capital.

MSci Project — Using PINNs to Solve Black Hole Perturbations

Sep 2025 – Apr 2026

Supervised by Dr. John Veitch, Institute of Gravitational Research · GitHub

  • Developed a Leapfrog numerical solver in Python for the time-domain Regge–Wheeler–Zerilli (RWZ) equation, extracting the dominant quasi-normal modes and grey-body factor of a Schwarzschild black hole.
  • Implemented a Physics-Informed Neural Network (PINN) framework that solves the RWZ equation, computes the quasi-normal mode spectrum, and mimics the in-going-only behaviour of the event horizon.
  • Authored a comprehensive master's dissertation comparing the numerical and machine-learning approaches, and presented the work to peers and senior academic staff.
